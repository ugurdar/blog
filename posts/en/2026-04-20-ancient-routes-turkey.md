Most of my work lives in data, models, and production systems. This project does not. It started with a much simpler urge: I love wandering through ancient cities.

There's something hard to explain about standing in the middle of a 2,000-year-old theatre, or following a road that traders, soldiers, and pilgrims walked centuries ago. Anatolia is *absurdly* rich in this — you can drive an hour in almost any direction and stumble onto ruins most countries would build a national museum around. The problem was always the same: *where do I actually go this weekend, and in what order?*

![Aizanoi ancient city at night, January 2026](/blog/ar/aizanoi.jpg)

*Aizanoi, January 2026 — wandering an ancient city alone on a snowy winter night. This is the feeling the whole project is trying to make easier to find.*

So I built **[Ancient Routes Türkiye](https://ancientroutesturkiye.com)** — part travel tool, part excuse to spend more time among old stones.

![Ancient Routes Türkiye — the landing page](/blog/ar/home.jpg)

*The landing page: explore ~140 ancient cities, plan a route, or pick a suggested trip.*

## What it does

The site is an interactive map of roughly **140 ancient cities across Anatolia**, built for the way I actually plan trips:

- **Explore** — browse the ancient cities visually on a map, not buried in a list.
- **Plan a route** — pick a start and an end point and get a custom itinerary through the sites in between.
- **Suggested routes** — ready-made trips for when you don't want to plan, like *İzmir → Ephesus* or *Antalya → Side*.
- **Find what's near you** — geolocation surfaces the ruins around wherever you happen to be.

For example, here's a real plan from **Eskişehir to Antalya** — the planner threads a route through the ancient cities along the way and gives you the distance, the time, and a few alternative routes to choose from:

The whole idea is to make Anatolia's ancient heritage feel like it's *at your fingertips* — something you go and experience, not just read about.

## Why I made it

Two reasons, honestly.

The first is personal. I've always been drawn to history, and visiting ancient cities is one of my favourite ways to spend a weekend. I wanted a tool that matched how I actually think about a trip: *start here, end there, and show me everything worth stopping for along the way.*

The second is that side projects keep me honest as a builder. This one pulled me onto the front-end and maps side — interactive UI, geolocation, routing — which isn't where I usually spend my time, and there was no deadline or stakeholder in sight. It's the kind of project where the only requirement is that *I* would use it. And I do.

## Try it

If you're ever in Türkiye with a free weekend and a bit of curiosity, give it a go: **[ancientroutesturkiye.com](https://ancientroutesturkiye.com)**. Pick a city, plot a route, and go stand somewhere very, very old.

And if you find a site I'm missing — and there are always more — tell me. There's no such thing as too many ruins.
